Day One Sunday, November 1

Gunma — The Beginning

Tatebayashi · Kawaba

```
🍱 Lunch 12:00 – 17:00

Sushi Obana

鮨おばな

5-1 Ōtemachi, Tatebayashi, Gunma

OAD #72 Tabelog Silver

The anchor of the entire trip. Chef Terukuni Obana trained at Kyubey in Ginza before returning to his hometown of Tatebayashi to open one of Japan's most quietly revered sushi counters. He sources his fish personally from Tsukiji each morning — the omakase unfolds as eleven courses of otsumami followed by twenty pieces of hand-formed nigiri. Sunday is the only day open for lunch.

Open hours: Sunday only · Lunch 12:00–17:00
Dinner Tue–Sat 17:30–21:30 · Closed Monday
Reserve via Pocket Concierge or Instagram →
1 hour west via Route 50 → Kawaba, Gunma
🍽 Dinner From 18:00

VENTINOVE

ヴェンティノーヴェ

2593-1 Yachi, Kawaba, Tone District, Gunma

Tabelog Bronze

Set on the grounds of the Tsuchida Sake Brewery in mountain Kawaba, VENTINOVE opened in 2022 and has already become one of Japan's most talked-about destination restaurants. Chef Yusuke Takeuchi cooks everything over wood fire, sourcing all ingredients from within a thirty-minute radius — foraged mushrooms, black truffles, hunted game, and the brewery's own sake lees in unexpected preparations. The Akagi beef bistecca is not to be missed.

Highly recommended: stay overnight as the auberge accepts one group per night, transforming this into a full immersive evening in the mountains.

Open hours: Dinner every day · Book via 29ventinove.com
Book at 29ventinove.com →
SleepVENTINOVE Auberge, Kawaba — or Numata town nearby

```
🍂 Morning Stop 09:00 – 11:30

Nikkō Tōshōgū

日光東照宮

2301 Sannai, Nikkō, Tochigi

UNESCO World Heritage Rating 4.5 · 33,000+ reviews

Depart Kawaba at 8:30 AM and arrive at Nikkō in one hour — perfectly timed for the 9:00 opening. November is the absolute peak of kōyō (紅葉) season here: the ancient cedar avenues, five-storey pagodas, and vermillion shrine gates are framed in blazing crimson and gold. Allow ninety minutes to two hours to walk the complex without rushing.

Open hours: Daily 09:00–16:00 · Admission ¥1,300
toshogu.jp →
2.5 hours via Nikkō IC → Tohoku Expressway → Sendai
🍱 Lunch 11:30 – 15:00

IL PIZZAIOLO

イル・ピッツァイオーロ

1-9-21 Tsutsumimachi, Aoba Ward, Sendai

Tabelog Bronze

A small, wood-fired counter in central Sendai serving what many consider the finest Neapolitan pizza in the Tohoku region. The dough ferments for 48 hours, the San Marzano tomatoes are imported directly, and the knowledgeable staff will guide you through sake and natural wine pairings. Arrive by 13:00 for a relaxed lunch.

Open hours: Monday, Friday–Sunday · Lunch 11:30–15:00 · Dinner 18:00–22:00
Closed Tuesday–Thursday
🍽 Dinner 18:00 – 23:00

Keisuke Matsumoto

松本圭介

2-3-17 Kashiwagi, Aoba Ward, Sendai

Five counter seats. That is all. Chef Matsumoto's philosophy — Japanese spirit harmonised with classical Western technique — produces dishes of extraordinary precision: white asparagus wrapped in Sendai Wagyu with spring truffle, Hidakami beef with a Shaoxing wine reduction, local shellfish in dashi beurre blanc. One of Sendai's most coveted tables; reserve months in advance.

Open hours: Daily 18:00–23:00 · Dinner only
Reserve via Tablecheck →
SleepSendai city — Hotel Metropolitan or Westin Sendai recommended

Day Three Tuesday, November 3 — Culture Day

Akayu — The Auberge

Nanyo, Yamagata

```

A gentle day. Drive one hour from Sendai to Akayu Onsen — one of Yamagata's oldest hot spring towns, known for its sulphurous waters and slow pace. Check in early, explore the historic bathhouses along the Matsukawa river, and rest before the evening.

🍽 Dinner From 18:00

Stanza della SINCERITA

オステリア シンチェリータ

3005 Akayu, Nanyō, Yamagata

Michelin Key Tabelog Bronze

Two rooms. One chef. Chef Makoto Harada built this intimate auberge in the heart of the Akayu Onsen district to express a single idea: Yamagata's seasons on a plate, cooked over wood fire in an Italian language. Yonezawa beef, mountain vegetables, housemade pasta, local sake — the evening meal here is unhurried and deeply personal. Staying overnight is the full experience; breakfast the following morning is equally considered.

Pricing: Overnight with two meals from ¥77,000–¥110,000 per person. Dinner only ¥33,000.

Open hours: Check website — advance booking essential
Book at osteria-sincerita.com →

Dewa Ya

出羽屋

〒990-0703 山形県西村山郡西川町間沢58, Nishikawa

Tabelog Silver Tabelog Chefs' Gold

At the foot of sacred Mt. Gassan, this legendary mountain inn has been serving the region's most extraordinary wild-foraged cuisine for generations. Chef Haruki Sato personally gathers the ingredients — mountain vegetables, wild mushrooms, bear meat, venison — and serves one group at a time in a setting of austere natural beauty. This is one of the hardest reservations in all of Japan; begin trying months in advance.

Open hours: Wed–Mon, Lunch only 11:30–14:00 · Closed Tuesday

Restaurant UOZEN

レストラン ウオゼン

1-10-69-8 Higashiōsaki, Sanjo, Niigata

OAD #180 Tabelog Silver

Chef Kazuhiro Inoue holds a hunting licence. He personally sources wild boar, deer, bear, and seasonal seafood from the Sea of Japan — then serves it as refined, inventive modern French cuisine in a converted farmhouse surrounded by rice paddies. The contrast between the pastoral setting and the precision of the cooking is part of what makes UOZEN one of Japan's most quietly significant restaurants.

Open hours: Tue–Sun · Dinner 17:30–23:00 (Wed–Sat also Lunch 11:30–15:00)

Murakami — Samurai Town

```
🍱 Lunch 11:30 – 14:30

Kappou Shintaku

割烹 新多久

3-38 Komachi, Murakami, Niigata

OAD #369 Tabelog Silver

Founded in 1867 and now run by brothers who trained in Kyoto's finest kaiseki houses, Shintaku draws over ninety percent of its ingredients from the Murakami region. This means the city's celebrated local salmon — salted and dried in the winter winds — appears in multiple forms; alongside wagyu, wild boar, and seasonal mountain vegetables. The historic kappou dining room, with its lacquered counter and shoji screens, is itself an experience.

After lunch, spend the afternoon walking Murakami's beautifully preserved samurai streets, visiting the sake breweries, and the city's renowned Masamune Iguchi Museum of Art before heading south.

Open hours: Mon–Tue, Thu–Sun · Lunch 11:30–14:30 · Dinner 17:00–21:30
Closed Wednesday
murakami-sintaku.com →
SleepMurakami, or drive south to Niigata city for accommodation

🍱 Final Lunch From 13:00

Tokyo — Your Choice

Tokyo

Arrive in Tokyo by midday for a final lunch of your choosing. After six days of destination dining, the city feels both familiar and electric. A few fitting ways to close the trip:

For sushi — Sushi Saito (Akasaka, if you can secure it), Sushi Yoshitake (Ginza, 3 Michelin stars), or Harutaka (Ginza).
For something celebratory — Den (Jingumae), one of Japan's most inventive and joyful meals.
For simplicity — a bowl of cold soba at Kanda Yabu Soba, Tokyo's oldest, founded 1880.

⚠ Note — Tokiwa Sushi Shibata Honten (登喜和鮨 新発田本店) This OAD #194 sushi counter in Shibata, Niigata — one of the most acclaimed outside Tokyo — operates its Honten location on weekends only (Sat–Sun, lunch from 11:30). The only Sunday in the trip is committed to Sushi Obana. Consider extending the trip by one day to add a Saturday, November 7 lunch in Shibata on the drive home from Murakami — it sits directly on the route.
